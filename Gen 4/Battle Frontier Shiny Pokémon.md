Multiple Battle Frontier facilities in Generation 4 feature ways to generate shiny Pokémon, despite GameFreak trying to prevent this with a shiny lock.
# Diamond and Pearl Battle Tower
In Pokémon Diamond and Pearl, there is a 1 in 204,800 chance of any Battle Tower Pokémon being shiny. This is due to a mistake in the code, where the game checks for both the Pokémon's nature and shiny status. If the generated Pokémon happens to have the correct nature (1 in 25) and is shiny (1 in 8192), the shiny lock is bypassed.

# Battle Arcade and Castle
In Pokémon Platinum, HeartGold, and SoulSilver, there is a 1 in 8,192 chance of any Battle Arcade or Castle Pokémon being shiny. While the shiny lock does work properly for these facilities, it is undone due to a mistake in the code. This mistake overrides all opponent's Pokémon's Trainer ID and Secret ID with the player's Trainer ID and Secret ID.
# Battle Factory
In Pokémon Platinum, HeartGold, and SoulSilver, the shiny lock works as intended, unless the Rest feature is used. Upon resting and reloading, previously generated Pokémon Trainer ID and Secret ID are rerolled without activating the shiny lock mechanism. After resting, Pokémon have a 1 in 8,192 chance to be shiny. This impacts both your rented Pokémon and Pokémon you haven't rented yet.
